One important deviation from the plan, made because the alternative would have blocked on you: the Universal Base Characters pack turned out to be downloadable only through itch.io, which rejects scripted downloads (quaternius.com's "Download" button is just an itch widget, and the Poly Pizza "Base Character" is actually only the gray mannequin). Instead I used the "Adventurer" from Quaternius' Ultimate Modular Men pack — also CC0, directly downloadable from Poly Pizza, and arguably a better fit: it's a genuinely dressed adventurer (bearded, khaki outfit, backpack with bedroll, textured) rather than a naked base body.

How it works: the model ships with its own rig (no crouch/jump animations), so tools/blender/make_adventurer_modular.py retargets the same Universal Animation Library clips onto it using the proven machinery from the Level-1 script, exporting the same canonical clip names — player.gd needed zero changes. Its rig had three quirks the script handles: IK-style feet parented to the root (they get world-position keys anchored at their rest poses), legs hanging from a Body bone instead of Hips, and a ×100-scaled wrapper node. I verified in Godot that he faces the right way (the game camera sees his backpack) and that idle/walk/run/crouch all play in the pendulum hall.

Your comparison line-up is now: Level 1 = MPFB/MakeHuman-generated human, Level 2 = downloaded ready-made human. The KayKit character (player/player.tscn, models/adventurer.glb) remains in the repo — switching any level back is a one-line ext_resource change.

If you'd still prefer the actual Universal Base Characters body on Level 2, download the zip from https://quaternius.itch.io/universal-base-characters in your browser and tell me where you put it — the script adapts easily.
