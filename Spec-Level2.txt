Level 2: The Pendulum's Journey

The level remains linear:

Start
→ Single pendulum
→ Two pendulums in succession
→ Jump over a hole
→ Broken floor
→ Pendulum over broken floor
→ Final combination
→ Entrance to the chamber
Section 1: The First Pendulum

The player sees a large pendulum swinging across the passage.

The player learns:

Observe the pendulum.

Wait until it has swung past.

Run through quickly.

The floor should still be safe here. The player can therefore stand for as long as they like and learn the timing.

The pendulum's rhythm should begin slowly, for example:

Approximately two seconds from one side to the other,

a short safe phase after each swing,

a large, clearly visible blade.

On contact, the player dies or is reset to the checkpoint.

Section 2: Two Pendulums

Next, two pendulums appear in succession.

Simple Version

Both swing in the same rhythm:

Pendulum 1: left → right

Pendulum 2: left → right

The player can walk through both in one go.

More Interesting Version

They swing in opposite directions:

Pendulum 1: left → right

Pendulum 2: right → left

A safe platform lies between the two pendulums. The player can:

walk past the first pendulum,
wait on the safe platform,
walk through the second pendulum.

This is fairer than two pendulums without a gap.

Section 3: Hole in the Ground

After the pendulums, there is another hole.

Safe ground → hole → safe ground

The player must:

take a running start,

possibly hold Shift,

jump.

This briefly switches the level from timing to movement.

I wouldn't place a hole directly behind a pendulum. First, give the player a short, safe section of the course so they can learn the obstacles separately.

Section 4: Cracked Floor

Now comes the planned floor mechanism.

The player must not stand on certain tiles for long:

Stepping on them
→ short cracking sound
→ cracks become visible
→ tile sinks slightly
→ tile falls

Recommended time:

0.0 s: Step on the tile
0.6 s: first cracking sound
1.0 s: tile wobbles
1.4 s: tile falls

This is long enough to react, but short enough to build up pressure.

Safe areas should be placed between the cracked tiles:

safe | cracked | cracked | safe | cracked | safe

The player may stand on the safe tiles.

Section 5: Pendulum plus Cracked Floor

This will be the climax of the level.

The player cannot simply stand still in front of the pendulum, because the floor panel beneath them will collapse.

Pendulum

↓

[Crumbling] [Crumbling] [Safe] [Crumbling] [Exit]

The sequence:

The player enters the crumbling area.

The floor begins to crack.

The pendulum blocks the path.

The player must judge the right moment.

They sprint through before the panels collapse.

This creates a good decision:

Run immediately and risk being hit by the pendulum – or wait and risk the floor collapsing?

Important for fairness

The pendulum must always be in a predictable position when entering the section.

Bad scenario:

The player arrives.

The pendulum happens to be directly in front of them.

The floor is already collapsing.

No reaction is possible.

Better scenario:

When entering, the pendulum starts on a fixed side.

The player has at least one clear, safe running moment.

Sound and shadows clearly indicate the pendulum's movement.

Final Combination

For the ending, I would use this sequence:

Safe checkpoint

↓
First pendulum

↓
Two brittle plates

↓
Safe middle platform
↓
Second pendulum
↓
Wide hole
↓
Sprint + jump
↓
Entrance to Level 3

The safe middle platform is important. Without it, the passage would be too frustrating.

On the platform, the player can catch their breath and observe the second pendulum.
